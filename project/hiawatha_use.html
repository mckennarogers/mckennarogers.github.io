About the Hiawatha Trail

About the Hiawatha Trail

Located in Northern Idaho, the Hiawatha Biking Trail is an old railroad turned into a Mountain Biking trail.

  • State: Idaho
  • When to Go: July - September
  • Supplies: A mountain bike! You can bring yours or rent!
  • Best Meal: Uncrustable at the end of the trail!
  • Advice: Most people will only go down the trail, but if you're up for a challenge, go back up! Dress warm for the tunnels, they are a bit chilly! Oh, also bring your own bike light. They are provided, but are low quality.
Hiawatha Story

My Hiawatha Story

I was able to experience this trip with my parents and two younger siblings in August of 2022.

We stayed in a cute Airbnb about 30 minutes away from the trailhead. There are a couple hotels, but Northern Idaho is scattered with very small towns.

We chose to rent mountain bikes rather than bring our own because it would be quite a lot to haul our five bikes all the way to Northern Idaho. We began the trail in the morning and cruised down! The views were absolutely breathtaking! Once we got to the bottom, we had a lovely lunch with squirrels and chipmunks begging for our food.

After lunch, we chose to ride back up the trail. This definitely took a lot longer than riding down. My mother and I did the majority of the ride up together while my siblings and father were behind us. Once my brother caught up with us, we started to pick up some speed! It was such an experience to finally finish!
